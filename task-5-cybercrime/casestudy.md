# Task 5 - Part A: Cybercrime Case Study (UPI Fraud)

**Crime Type:** UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**Overview:**
UPI fraud is a rapidly growing crime in India, specifically targeting young students and digital payment users. It typically involves social engineering where the attacker tricks the victim into authorizing a transaction.

**How it Happens (Step-by-Step):**
1. The attacker lists a fake product (like a second-hand laptop or hostel furniture) on an online marketplace at a very low price.
2. When the victim shows interest, the attacker claims they are unable to meet in person and suggests using UPI.
3. Instead of sending money, the attacker sends a "Collect Request" or a QR code to the victim, claiming it is for "verification" or to "receive" a deposit.
4. The victim, under pressure or lack of technical awareness, enters their UPI PIN. 
5. Entering the PIN authorizes the debit, and money is instantly transferred from the victim’s account to the attacker.

**Target & Consequences:**
College students are frequent targets due to their high usage of digital wallets and peer-to-peer marketplaces. The consequences include immediate financial loss, emotional distress, and potential identity theft if personal bank details were shared during the process.
